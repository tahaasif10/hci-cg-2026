Biography

Don Norman is a pioneer in the field of Human–Computer Interaction (HCI). He is best known for his book The Design of Everyday Things, where he introduced concepts like affordances, signifiers, and the Action Cycle.

His work focuses on bridging the Gulf of Execution and the Gulf of Evaluation, ensuring that technology is designed with the user's psychology in mind.
